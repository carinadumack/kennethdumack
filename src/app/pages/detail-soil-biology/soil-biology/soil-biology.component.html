Protists are the main consumers of soil bacteria and fungi, adding to nutrient cycling and suppression of plant diseases. I investigate biotic interactions among the vast microbial diversity in soils. For this, I use -omics techniques, bioinformatics, and also microscopy-based approaches.

Selected research

The Plant Microbiome

Plants regulate attract certain soil bacteria from their environment. However, the presense and increased activity of this bacterial community attracts their predators, protists, too! Currently our research is funded by the DFG SPP 1991!

Fungivorous protists

Protists were long not recognized as consumers of soil fungi. We are working to explore the mechanisms that fungivorous protists evolved and the extant this process has on soil- and plant biology.

Soil formation in the Arctic and Antarctica

Soils in the polar regions are special. They are frequently frozen and are uninhabitable for the majority of plants. Instead, these soils are covered by highly diverse biological soil crusts. I am currently investigating the biological interactions within. Obviously, these communities are threatened by climate change. Currently our research is funded by the DFG SPP 1058!
